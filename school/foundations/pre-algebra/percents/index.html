The Sale Tag Problem

A $80 jacket is 25% off. How much do you save? How much do you pay?

Most people panic. But here's the truth: 25% just means 25 out of 100. That's a ratio. You already know how to handle ratios.

25% of $80 = $20 saved. You pay $60. Let's build the hack to always get there fast.

② The Hack

Percent = Part ÷ Whole × 100

Three things are always in play: the part, the whole, and the percent. Know any two, find the third.

⚡ The Hack — The Percent Triangle
Percent = (Part ÷ Whole) × 100
Part = (Percent ÷ 100) × Whole
Whole = Part ÷ (Percent ÷ 100)
÷
Find the percent
Part ÷ Whole × 100
×
Find the part
(% ÷ 100) × Whole
÷
Find the whole
Part ÷ (% ÷ 100)
Example: 30% of 150 = ?
(30 ÷ 100) × 150 = 0.3 × 150 = 45

Example: 18 is what % of 72?
(18 ÷ 72) × 100 = 0.25 × 100 = 25%
