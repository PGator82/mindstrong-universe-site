Why Hexagons Rule Nature

Honeybees build hexagonal cells. Why not squares or triangles? Hexagons pack perfectly with no wasted space and use the least material. Nature optimizes geometry.

Here's the mind-blowing part: add up all interior angles of ANY hexagon — regular or irregular — and you always get 720°. Every time. The formula never fails.

② The Hack

One Formula Rules All Polygons

Any polygon can be split into triangles by drawing diagonals from one vertex. A polygon with n sides makes (n − 2) triangles. Each triangle has 180°.

⚡ Interior Angle Sum Formula
Sum = (n − 2) × 180°
where n = number of sides

Pentagon (5): (5−2)×180 = 540°
Hexagon (6): (6−2)×180 = 720°
Octagon (8): (8−2)×180 = 1080°
Each angle (regular polygon)
= (n−2)×180 ÷ n

= 360° (any polygon)

Quadrilaterals (4 sides) always sum to (4−2)×180 = 360°. That covers squares, rectangles, parallelograms, rhombuses, and trapezoids.
