The Coordinate Plane

Two number lines, crossed at right angles. Every point in space gets an address. This is where algebra meets geometry.

Mode:
① Hook

René Descartes, Lying in Bed

Legend has it that René Descartes invented the coordinate plane while sick in bed, watching a fly on his ceiling. He realized he could describe the fly's exact position with just two numbers — how far across and how far up.

That idea — every point gets a unique address (x, y) — changed mathematics forever. It's how GPS works. It's how video games render every pixel. It's the bridge between geometry and algebra.

Quadrants + Distance Formula

⚡ Read Any Point

⚡ Distance Formula
Distance = √[(x₂−x₁)² + (y₂−y₁)²]
It's just Pythagoras — the horizontal and vertical gaps form a right triangle.
