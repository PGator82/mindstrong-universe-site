The Clock Problem

At 3:00, the clock hands form a perfect right angle — exactly 90°. At 6:00, they form a straight line — 180°. At 12:00, they overlap — 0°.

Every time you look at a clock, you're reading angles. Every building, bridge, and screen you see is a network of lines. Let's learn the language.

Right
Exactly 90°
Acute

Obtuse

② The Hack

Angle Pairs — The Two Rules That Run Everything

Two rules cover almost every angle problem you'll ever see:

⚡ Rule 1 — Supplementary
Angles on a straight line add to 180°
If one angle is 110°, the other is 70°
⚡ Rule 2 — Complementary
Angles in a right angle add to 90°
If one angle is 35°, the other is 55°
Vertical Angles

Opposite angles when two lines cross are always equal. X = X.

Parallel Lines

Cut by a transversal — alternate interior angles are equal, co-interior add to 180°.
